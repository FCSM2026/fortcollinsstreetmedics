Our Movement

Fort Collins Street Medics are neighbors and activists focused on providing free medical training, harm reduction, and mutual aid for those facing barriers to traditional healthcare.

We are a grassroots, volunteer-run mutual aid collective. Our role is to promote safety, provide first aid at social justice actions, and support community autonomy through direct action.

Action First Aid

Treating irritants, scene safety, and rapid response in high-stress environments.

Wound Care

Hemorrhage control, infection prevention, and field bandaging techniques.

Community Care

De-escalation, hydration support, and preventative health education for all.

FAQ

Do I need medical experience to join? ▼
No prior experience is required. We believe everyone can learn life-saving skills. Our trainings are designed for community members of all backgrounds and skill levels.
Are your trainings certified? ▼
Our focus is on practical, community-based skills. While our practices align with recognized standards (like Stop the Bleed), our goal is preparedness and accessibility, not formal certification.
How do I get involved? ▼
Reach out via the contact form, Facebook, or Instagram. To volunteer at events, we require members to complete at least one of our training sessions or have prior street medic experience.
Are you a non-profit? ▼
No. We are a grassroots, volunteer-run mutual aid collective. We operate without the restrictions or bureaucracy of 501(c)(3) structures.
